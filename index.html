Plantae bettinae

Plants extracted by Ferdinand Lindheimer near the nascent colony of Bettina located in the unceded hunting grounds of the Penateka Comanche. Named and described by Asa Gray or George Engelman based on cut and dried samples sent to them by Lindheimer. Retrace their images with the arrows of the Adelsverein. Listen for the sound of the Llano River and the call of the Golden-cheeked warbler. Consider which of the three plants was brought here by white settlers to kill fish. Consider which might have edible roots. Consider why exactly Herr Lindheimer was paid to cut them.
